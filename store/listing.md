## English (en-US)

### Short description (80 chars)

`SSH, SFTP, FTP & terminal for foldables. Open source. No tracking. No ads.`
*(74 chars)*

### Long description (~3950 chars)

Ori:Dev (折り Dev) is the SSH, SFTP and terminal client built for the Pixel Fold and modern Android foldables. It puts a real developer workstation in your pocket — and when you unfold the screen, it gives you a true dual-pane workspace.

**Multi-protocol connectivity**
Connect to any Linux, BSD or Unix host over SSH, SFTP, FTP or FTPS. SSHJ powers fast and reliable sessions, while Apache Commons Net handles legacy FTP servers. Save unlimited connection profiles, organise them into folders, and tag them by environment (dev, staging, prod, homelab).

**A real terminal, not a toy**
The terminal is built on ConnectBot's battle-tested termlib and ships with full xterm-256color support, true mouse reporting, scrollback buffer, copy-paste with selection, named sessions, and tmux-friendly keybindings. htop, vim, nano, neovim, btop and weechat all work the way you expect.

**Dual-pane file manager**
On a Pixel Fold inner display, the file manager shows two panes side by side — drag and drop between local and remote, or between two remote hosts. On a phone, swipe between panes. Bulk select, rename, chmod, chown, symlink, and inline preview for text, images and PDFs.

**Code editor with AI**
Edit remote files in place using Sora-Editor, with syntax highlighting for 80+ languages, line numbers, soft wrap, and a built-in git diff view that shows your changes against the last saved version. Optional Claude AI integration lets you select code and ask for refactors, explanations or fixes — your snippets are only sent when you explicitly ask.

**Transfer manager**
A dedicated transfer queue handles uploads and downloads in the background, with pause, resume, retry, throttling, and per-host concurrency limits. SFTP transfers survive screen-off and Doze.

**Proxmox dashboard**
Manage your Proxmox VE cluster from your phone: list nodes, start/stop/reboot VMs and LXC containers, take snapshots, view resource graphs, and open a serial console straight into the terminal — all from one screen.

**Wear OS companion**
The Pixel Watch 2 companion app monitors your active SSH sessions, lets you fire pre-saved quick commands, and offers a panic disconnect tile that closes every session in one tap when you need to walk away from a sensitive shell.

**Security first**
All credentials are stored in the Android Keystore using AES-256-GCM. Biometric unlock is optional but recommended. SSH host keys follow Trust on First Use and any mismatch is rejected hard. Passwords are held as char arrays and zeroed after use. The clipboard is marked sensitive and auto-cleared after 30 seconds.

**100% open source. No tracking. No ads. No premium gates.**
Ori:Dev is licensed under the Apache 2.0 license. Crash reports are opt-in and anonymised. There are no analytics SDKs, no third-party trackers, no upsells. Every feature is available to every user, forever.

Built by a developer, for developers. Designed in Indigo `#6366F1`.

Verbinde dich mit jedem Linux-, BSD- oder Unix-Host über SSH, SFTP, FTP oder FTPS. SSHJ sorgt für schnelle, stabile Sessions, Apache Commons Net unterstützt klassische FTP-Server. Speichere beliebig viele Verbindungsprofile, organisiere sie in Ordnern und markiere sie nach Umgebung (dev, staging, prod, homelab).

Das Terminal basiert auf der bewährten ConnectBot-Bibliothek termlib und liefert vollständigen xterm-256color-Support, Maus-Reporting, Scrollback-Puffer, Copy-Paste mit Selektion, benannte Sessions und tmux-freundliche Tastenbelegungen. htop, vim, nano, neovim, btop und weechat funktionieren genau so, wie du es erwartest.

Auf dem Smartphone wechselst du per Wischgeste. Mehrfachauswahl, Umbenennen, chmod, chown, Symlinks und Inline-Vorschau für Text, Bilder und PDFs.
